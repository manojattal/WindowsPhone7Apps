Weather Summary
Kari Kiefer
Weather Underground Forecast for Sunday, December 11, 2011.

High pressure will bring another quiet day to Asia on Sunday. A large and dominant ridge of high pressure will continue building over Mongolia and China. This will continue pushing moisture away, allowing for another sunny day. However, flow from the north will keep temperatures cool. Beijing and Shanghai will remain sunny with highs in the mid-40s(F)/ near 6(C), while Hong Kong will see a few patchy clouds with highs in the lower 60s(F)/ mid-teens(C).

To the east, a large low pressure system in the Bering Sea will continue pulling eastward and further away from eastern Asia. The back side of this system will continue creating strong flow from the north, pushing moisture into Japan from the Sea of Japan. This will kick up some rain showers with high elevation snow showers. Tokyo will be sunny and cool with highs in the upper 50s(F)/ mid-teens(C).

In Australia, the remnants of Tropical Cyclone Alenga will continue approaching Western Australia. This will kick up scattered coastal showers throughout the day, becoming heavier in the evening. Meanwhile in the east, a low pressure system over Queensland and New South Whales will produce widespread showers and thunderstorms from Melbourne to Cairns. Sydney will see scattered thunderstorms with highs in the lower 80s(F)/ near 27(C). High pressure over the Great Australian Bight and central Australia will produce another hot and dry day.
